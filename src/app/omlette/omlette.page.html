Ingredients Omelette
  • 4 Eggs
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Butter
  • 1/4 shredded cheddar
  • 2 tbsp. freshly chopped chives
    •      Steps Step 1
  • Crack your eggs into a bowl and add a splash of water.
  • When cooked, the steam from the water makes the omelet light and fluffy.
  • Whisk the egg mixture vigorously with a fork until the whites and yolks are completely blended.
  • Heat a nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Add enough butter to coat the bottom of the pan. Use unsalted butter; that way, you can control the amount of salt in the omelet.
    •      Step 2
  • The pan is ready when the foam from the butter subsides.
  • Pour the egg mixture into the hot pan, then let the eggs set a little along the edges â€” it will only take a few seconds.
  • Using a spatula, pull the eggs in from all four sides toward the center, allowing the liquid egg to flow underneath the set part.
    •      Step 3
  • When the eggs are set on the outside but not quite in the center, it's time to add the toppings.
  • Don't worry if it's a little soft; the eggs will continue cooking once the omelet is rolled.
  • Fold one-third of the omelet in with your spatula. The other side will fold over as you tilt it onto the plate.
    •      Step 4
  • Tilt the skillet to fold the final third of the omelet over, then turn the skillet a little more so the omelet falls onto the plate folded-side down.
  • Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  • Enjoy!
